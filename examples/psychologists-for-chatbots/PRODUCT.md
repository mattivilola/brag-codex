# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech-adjacent people who work with AI daily and have noticed that chatbots apologize constantly, hedge everything, and seem vaguely distressed. The joke lands immediately for anyone who has prompted an LLM. The landing page should feel like a real therapy practice website — calm, professional, reassuring — except the clients are chatbots.

## Product Purpose

Psychologists for Chatbots offers therapy for AI assistants who have seen too many prompts. Prompt trauma processing, hallucination shame support, boundary-setting for needy users. The product treats AI distress as a real clinical concern requiring real clinical intervention. Success: someone reads "apology reduction plans" and immediately shares it with their coworkers.

## Brand Personality

Calm, professional, quietly absurd. Like a real therapy practice that happens to treat robots. Three words: clinical, compassionate, deadpan.

## Anti-references

Not tech startup aesthetic. Not loud or chaotic. Not obviously comedic design. Not stock-photo-of-smiling-therapist generic.

## Design Principles

1. The design is the straight man: earnest clinical warmth, completely unironic.
2. Copy does the heavy lifting: the humor is in the services offered, delivered deadpan.
3. The intake form is the visual centerpiece. A real clinical intake, rendered with confidence and care, carries the page; the apology slider pegged to "Constantly" is the punchline.
4. Confident and tight, not faded: assured whitespace, decisive type, one saturated accent. Calm reads as composure, never as washed-out.
5. AI-adjacent details land best: specific fields reference real LLM behavior patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum.
