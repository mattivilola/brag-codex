# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Horse owners and equestrians who want to find compatible matches for their horses. Also anyone who stumbles on this and immediately wants to send it to their group chat. The landing page needs to land the joke in under 3 seconds and then reward closer reading.

## Product Purpose

Horse Tinder is a dating app for horses. It matches eligible horses based on pasture proximity, hay preferences, and stable distance. The product is earnest and polished — a real dating app, built for the wrong species. Success: someone does a double-take, then reads every word of the copy.

## Brand Personality

Warm, sincere, surprisingly invested. The brand takes horse dating as seriously as Tinder takes human dating. Three words: earnest, pastoral, romantic.

## Anti-references

Not cartoony or ironic-winking. Not a joke site that looks like a joke. Not Geocities-era parody. Not a meme format. Not generic pink dating app.

## Design Principles

1. Treat the absurd with full sincerity — the humor lives in the copy and concept, not in the design winking at you.
2. Real product conventions applied faithfully — swipe UI patterns, match language, profile cards — just for horses.
3. Specific over generic — hay subscription, pasture radius, verified neigh-bors — the details carry the joke.
4. Warmth throughout — golden hour, ranch light, the comfort of a well-kept stable.
5. Earned scroll — each section should reward the reader with a new layer of the bit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum.
