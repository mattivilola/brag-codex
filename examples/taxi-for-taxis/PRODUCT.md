# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who use ride-hailing apps and immediately understand the recursive joke. The landing page should look exactly like Uber or Lyft — clean, urban, map-forward, action-oriented — except the service dispatches larger taxis to pick up tired taxis.

## Product Purpose

Taxi for Taxis is ride-hailing for ride-hailing assets. When your taxi needs a ride. Cab-to-cab routing, surge pricing for tired vehicles, emergency pickup for emotionally unavailable sedans. The product solves a problem that only exists because someone thought of it. Success: someone reads "emotionally unavailable sedans" and has to put their phone down.

## Brand Personality

Urban, efficient, completely matter-of-fact. Like a real mobility startup that found a gap in the market and moved fast. Three words: confident, transactional, deadpan.

## Anti-references

Not yellow taxi cliché design. Not retro cab aesthetic. Not Lyft's overly friendly pink. Not heavy illustration.

## Design Principles

1. Ride-hailing UI conventions applied exactly — the familiar patterns make the absurdity sharper.
2. Operational specificity — dispatch language, routing terminology, fleet management copy.
3. Urban at night — city maps, streaked lights, the texture of a city that moves constantly.
4. Efficiency as tone — short sentences, action verbs, no warmth where a startup wouldn't have warmth.
5. The joke is structural — explain the product straight, let the reader discover the recursion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum.
